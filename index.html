1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This document aims to give a thorough summary of the conclusions and revelations drawn
from the information gathered throughout the advertising trial. The main goal is to offer a
thorough analysis of the findings and to make inferences from the information collected. The
report will summarize the main findings of the experiment, offer a thorough analysis of the
data, and make suggestions for further study or action. This experiment tested whether two
alternative forms of advertising could boost sales. 9,032 clients received an email from us,
and 4,516 others saw a pop-up window with a discount coupon on the website. Also, we
gathered data on each customer's age, gender, and level of loyalty. We wanted to know if
these advertising strategies affected sales, and which one performed best. We discovered
that pop-up advertisements didn't have the expected impact on sales after examining our ad
campaigns. Customers who saw the pop-up ads had generally lower sales and average sales
per customer than those who did not. Pop-up advertisements are therefore inefficient and
may not be the greatest strategy for upcoming campaigns.
Yet, email marketing was efficient at boosting sales. When compared to customers who
didn't open the email, some who did had much greater average sales per customer. Hence,
email advertising may be a useful outreach strategy for future initiatives. We also examined
demographic factors like age, gender, and level of loyalty, but we couldn't detect any
appreciable impacts on the efficiency of the advertising strategies. Based on our findings,
we highly recommend using email advertisements as a method of outreach in future
advertising campaigns.
2. OBJECTIVE AND QUESTIONS
The objective of analyzing the data from the email and pop-up advertisement tables is to
determine whether online advertisement has an impact on sales for PopRunner. This analysis
will help the company to understand the effectiveness of their marketing campaigns and
make informed decisions about how to allocate their resources to optimize sales. The
analysis will also help to identify any trends or patterns in consumer behavior that can inform
future marketing strategies. Ultimately, the goal is to improve the company's overall
performance and profitability by maximizing the return on investment for their marketing
efforts.

2.1.1. Objective of the project
The objective of this project is to use SQL to analyze the data from the email and pop-up
advertisement tables and determine the impact of online advertisement on sales for
PopRunner.
2.1.2. Questions
What is the correlation between the email blast and pop-up advertisement campaigns and
the resulting purchases made by consumers?
What are the key characteristics of consumers who are more likely to make a purchase
after being exposed to the email blast or pop-up advertisement campaigns?
Is there a significant difference in the conversion rates between the email blast and pop-
up advertisement campaigns, and if so, which campaign is more effective at driving sales for
PopRunner?
2.1.3. Data
Four tables were made available to us for this project: consumer, pop_up, email, and
purchase. The consumer table contains information about PopRunner's customers, including
their demographic data such as age, gender, and loyalty_status. The pop_up and email tables
contain data about the two types of advertisement campaigns conducted by PopRunner, with
the pop_up table containing data related to a pop-up advertisement with a Saved_discount
that was sent to selected customers, and the email table containing data related to an email
blast sent to all customers.
The pop-up advertisement campaign was targeted at specific customers who landed on
PopRunner's website and offered a discount code that could be used on the website. The
email blast campaign, on the other hand, was sent to all customers and contained a generic
message with the same discount code. Both campaigns aimed to incentivize customers to
make purchases on the website by offering them a discount on their orders.

3.3. Sales (Query 8, 10 and 12)
According to the customer analysis dataset, the average total sales for customers during
their online purchase is $135.2142.
In consideration of the data analysis, it was found that the pop-up advertisement did not
have a significant impact on increasing sales. The average sales amount total of customers
who received the pop-up message was lower than those who did not receive it. The customers
who did not receive the pop-up message had a higher average sales amount total and a higher
sum-total of sales compared to the customers who did receive the pop-up message.
Specifically, the customers who did not receive the pop-up message had an average sales
amount total of $138.6901 and a sum-total of sales of $626324.5. On the other hand, the
customers who did receive the pop-up message had an average sales amount total of
$131.7382 and a sum-total of sales of $594929.8.
The analysis of the email dataset suggests that the email blast did not have a significant
impact on the purchasing behavior of customers. The customers who opened the email had
a higher average sales amount of $240.8268, but a lower sum-total of sales of $172432
compared to customers who did not open the email. The customers who did not open the
email had a lower average sales amount of $126.1210 but a higher sum-total of sales of
$1048822.

3.4. Use cases (Query 11 and 13)
Based on the analysis of the database, it has been identified that the customer with the
customer ID 5887286353, who had the lowest expenditure at PopRunner, did not receive a
pop-up message on the website. This information highlights a potential missed opportunity
for PopRunner to engage with the customer and encourage them to make a purchase. Also,
the analysis of the dataset confirms that the customer with the customer ID 5955534353,
who had the highest expenditure at PopRunner, had opened the email that was sent to them.
This information highlights the effectiveness of the email marketing strategy employed by
PopRunner, as it resulted in a high-value purchase from the customer who engaged with the
email.
4. RECOMMENDATIONS AND CONCLUSION
After a detailed analysis on the data using the sqldf package in R, it was observed that
only 50% of the customers received the pop-up message, out of which only 33% saved
the discount. Moreover, the average sales of customers who did not receive or save the
discount were higher than those who did, indicating that the impact of the discount codes
was not successful.
However, the data also revealed that the average sales of customers who opened the email
blast were higher than those who did not, and the customer who spent the highest amount
did open the email blast. It was observed that only 716 customers opened the email blast,
indicating that efforts should be made to increase the awareness of the email blast among
customers.
Based on these insights, we recommend that PopRunner should focus on building
stronger relationships with customers in the 30-33 years age group, as they seem to have
a higher loyalty status. Additionally, the discount pop-ups should be made more appealing
to consumers to attract them and encourage them to save the discounts. Finally, efforts
should be made to increase the awareness of the email blast among customers to ensure
that more people open it, leading to positive results on overall sales.
In summary, the data analysis indicates that while the impact of the discount codes was
not successful, efforts should be made to increase customer engagement through other
means, such as the email blast, and to improve the effectiveness of discount pop-ups to
drive sales.
